Water Security
42% without water 84% of the urban population has access to water 32% of the rural population has access to water

Since the end of the Sierra Leone civil war, access to water has not improved, even declining in rural areas. Health problems arise from pollutants and poor sanitation from collecting drinking water from polluted sources.The government of Sierra leone has trouble maintaining their water supply. It struggles to ensure distribution to areas that require sufficient drinking water.

Food Security
Over 40% are food insecure Over 70% of the population lives below the national poverty line of $2 a day Over 40% of children suffer from Chronic Malnutrition

Due to weird rainfall patterns, rice production has been cut in half, so the government is spending millions of dollars to import rice. Total cereal supply in 2000, including rice in milled form, is estimated at 181 000 tonnes against a utilization requirement of 510 000 tonnes, resulting in an import requirement of 329 000 tonnes for 2000.
